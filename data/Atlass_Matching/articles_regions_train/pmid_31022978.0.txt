Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Olfactory Bulb**

2. **Piriform Cortex**

3. **Entorhinal Cortex**

4. **Hippocampus**

5. **Striatum**
   - Caudate nucleus
   - Putamen
   - Lentiform nucleus

6. **Thalamus**

7. **Amygdala**

8. **Hypothalamus**

9. **Cerebellum**

10. **Parahippocampal Gyrus**

11. **Insula**
   - Anterior insula
   - Posterior insula

12. **Frontal Cortex**
   - Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC)
   - Brodmann area 10 (BA 10)
   - Inferior frontal gyrus
   - Orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) BA 47

13. **Superior Temporal Gyrus**

14. **Fusiform Gyrus**

15. **Cingulate Gyrus**

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including:
- Olfactory processing (olfactory bulb, piriform cortex)
- Memory processing and consolidation (entorhinal cortex, hippocampus, parahippocampal gyrus)
- Reward processing (striatum, amygdala, orbitofrontal cortex)
- Energy regulation (hypothalamus)
- Motor control and coordination (cerebellum)
- Sensory processing (insula, thalamus)
- Executive functions and decision-making (frontal cortex, cingulate gyrus)
- Language and social cognition (superior temporal gyrus, fusiform gyrus)
