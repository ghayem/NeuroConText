Based on the neuroscientific article provided, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned:

1. **Visual Cortex**
   - Early visual cortex
   - Retinotopically organized regions of visual cortex
   - Striate and extrastriate cortex
   - V1
   - V2
   - V3/Vp
   - V3a/V4v

2. **Fronto-parietal Cortex**
   - Dorsal fronto-parietal cortex
   - Ventral fronto-parietal cortex
   - Middle frontal gyrus (MFG)
   - Intraparietal sulcus (IPS)
     - Left IPS
     - Right IPS

3. **Temporal Cortex**
   - Superior temporal gyrus
     - Right superior temporal gyrus corresponding to temporo-parietal junction (TPJ)
   - Ventral occipito-temporal cortex

4. **Parietal Cortex**
   - Precuneus

5. **Category-selective Cortex**
   - Fusiform face area (FFA)
     - Left FFA
     - Right FFA

6. **Prefrontal Cortex**
   - Precentral gyrus

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including:
- Visual processing and sensory adaptation (visual cortex)
- Attentional control and allocation (fronto-parietal cortex, superior temporal gyrus, precuneus)
- Processing of distractor items and faces (fusiform face area)
- Working memory and distractor filtering (prefrontal cortex)
