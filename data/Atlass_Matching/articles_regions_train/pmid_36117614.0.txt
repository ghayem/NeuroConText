Based on the article, the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned are:

1. **Primary somatosensory cortex**

2. **Secondary somatosensory cortex**

3. **Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)**

4. **Prefrontal cortex (PFC)**

5. **Insular cortex**
   - Inferior segment of the circular sulcus of the insula
   - Anterior insular cortex
   - Posterior insular cortex

6. **Amygdala**

7. **Thalamus**

8. **Cerebellum**

9. **Periaqueductal grey matter (PAG)**

10. **Inferior temporal sulcus**

11. **Inferior temporal gyrus (ITG)**
    - Left inferior temporal gyrus (T3)

12. **Middle temporal gyrus (MTG)**
    - Right middle temporal gyrus (T2)

These regions are involved in various aspects of pain processing, including sensing, modulation, and reaction to pain signals, as well as other cognitive and sensory functions.
