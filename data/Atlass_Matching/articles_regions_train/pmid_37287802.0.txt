Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article, formatted as requested:

1. **Default Mode Network (DMN)**
   - Posterior cingulate cortex (PCC)
   - Precuneus (PCu)
   - Inferior parietal lobe (IPL)
   - Left and right angular gyrus
   - Left and right medial frontal gyrus
   - Cingulate gyrus
   - Left and right para-hippocampal gyrus

2. **Central Executive Network (CEN)**
   - Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC)
   - Posterior parietal cortex (PPC)
   - Multiple medial-frontal regions
   - Anterior cingulate cortex (ACG)
   - Paracallosal cingulate
   - Right precentral gyrus
   - Right middle frontal gyrus
   - Right inferior parietal lobule
   - Suprafrontal gyrus (SFGmed.L)
   - Middle frontal gyrus (MFG.L)

3. **Salient Network (SN)**
   - Ventral lateral prefrontal cortex (VLPFC)
   - Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)
   - Left and right insula
   - Left and right middle frontal gyrus
   - Left cingulate gyrus

4. **Other Regions**
   - Hippocampal circuits
   - Cortical neurons
   - Subcortical regions
   - Auditory network
   - Sensorimotor network
   - Bilateral angular gyrus (AG)
   - Right middle frontal gyrus
   - Superior frontal gyrus

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including but not limited to: situational memory, visuospatial processing, consciousness, working memory, decision-making, retention, operational target detection, language, and executive functions.
