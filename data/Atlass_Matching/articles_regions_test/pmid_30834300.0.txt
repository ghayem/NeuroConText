Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the neuroscientific article:

1. **Cerebellum**
   - Left cerebellar hemisphere (lobules VI, VII, and VIIIa)
   - Bilateral paravermis area (lobule XI)

2. **Pre-supplementary motor area (pre-SMA)**
   - Extending to the dorsal tier of the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)

3. **Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)**
   - Dorsal tier (Brodmann’s areas 24c and 32)
   - Ventral (limbic) tier (Brodmann’s areas 24a and 24b, and subcallosal area 25)

4. **Anterior medial frontal cortex**

5. **Fusiform gyrus**

6. **Anterior superior temporal gyri**

7. **Posterior superior temporal gyri**

8. **Medial prefrontal cortex**

9. **Orbitofrontal cortex**

10. **Amygdala**

11. **Parietal lobe**

12. **Premotor cortex**

13. **Caudal part of the inferior frontal gyrus**

14. **Insula**

15. **Dorsal anterior insular cortex (dAIC)**

16. **Middle occipital gyrus (MOG)**

17. **Lateral occipitotemporal cortex (LOTC)**

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including social interaction, attention, emotion, motor control, and sensory processing.
