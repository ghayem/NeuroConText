Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Frontal Cortex**
   - Dorsal medial frontal cortex
   - Ventromedial prefrontal regions
   - Lateral superior and middle frontal cortices
   - Orbitofrontal cortex
   - Inferior frontal cortex
   - Superior frontal cortex
   - Medial frontal cortex
   - Middle frontal cortex
   - Anterior cingulate cortex

2. **Parietal Cortex**
   - Inferior parietal cortex
   - Precuneus
   - Superior parietal cortex
   - Lateral parietal cortex
   - Postcentral cortex
   - Supramarginal gyrus
   - Paracentral cortex
   - Posterior cingulate cortex

3. **Temporal Cortex**
   - Postcentral and superior temporal cortices
   - Inferior temporal cortex
   - Superior temporal sulcus
   - Superior temporal cortex
   - Calcarine cortex
   - Anterior inferior temporal cortex
   - Middle temporal cortex
   - Posterior superior temporal gyrus and sulcus (pSTG/S)
   - Temporo-parieto-occipital junction (TPO)

4. **Occipital Cortex**
   - Occipital pole
   - Lingual cortex

5. **Other Regions**
   - Corpus callosum (genu, body, splenium)
   - Insula
   - Central and postcentral sulci
   - Medial paracentral cortex
   - Cortical surface
   - Cortical vertices
   - Cortical gyrification
   - Cortical morphology
   - White matter
   - Grey matter
   - Cephalo-spinal fluid
   - Posterior part of the corpus callosum
   - Cortical space

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including but not limited to:
- Language
- Memory
- Executive functions
- Visual recognition
- Cognitive control
- Social cognition
- Mathematics and learning abilities
- Psychotic symptoms
