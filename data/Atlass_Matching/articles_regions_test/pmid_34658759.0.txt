Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the neuroscientific article:

1. **Default Mode Network (DMN)**
    - Posterior cingulate cortex (PCC)
    - Precuneus
    - Medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC)
    - Inferior parietal lobule (IPL)
    - Lateral temporal cortex
    - Temporal pole (TP)

2. **Executive-Control Network (EN)**
    - Dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC)
    - Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC)
    - Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)
    - Inferior frontal gyrus (IFG)
    - Orbital frontal cortex (OFC)
    - Middle frontal gyrus (MFG)

3. **Salience Network**
    - Dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC)
    - Insula

4. **Auditory Network**
    - Left middle temporal gyrus (MTG)
    - Right superior temporal gyrus (STG)
    - Planum temporale (PT)
    - Temporal pole (TP)

5. **Motor Network**
    - Bilateral supplementary motor cortex (SMA)
    - Right pre-supplementary motor area (pre-SMA)
    - Left cerebellum
    - Right precentral gyrus (preCG)
    - Dorsal premotor cortex (dPMC)
    - Ventral premotor cortex (vPMC)

6. **Other Regions**
    - Left inferior parietal lobule (IPL)
    - Angular gyrus (AG)
    - Supramarginal gyrus (SMG)
    - Medial frontal gyrus
    - Postcentral gyrus
    - Fusiform gyrus
    - Occipital lobe
    - Parietal lobe
    - Frontal lobe
    - Temporal lobe

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including creativity, auditory perception, motor control, executive functions, and default mode processes.
