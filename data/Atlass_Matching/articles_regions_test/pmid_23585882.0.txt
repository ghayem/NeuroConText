Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Striatum**
   - Caudate
   - Putamen

2. **Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)**
   - Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex (DLPFC)
     - Superior/Middle frontal gyrus (BA 8/9)
   - Ventrolateral Prefrontal Cortex (VLPFC)
     - Inferior frontal gyrus (BA 44, 47)
   - Ventromedial Prefrontal Cortex (BA 25)
   - Orbitofrontal Cortex
     - Gyrus rectus (BA 11)
   - Premotor Cortex (BA 6)

3. **Frontal Cortex**
   - Inferior Frontal Cortex (BA 47)
   - Premotor Cortex (BA 6)
   - Frontal Cortex (BA 8, 9, 10)

4. **Parietal Cortex**
   - Inferior Parietal Cortex (BA 40)
   - Precuneus (BA 7)
   - Parieto-occipital part of the Angular Gyrus (BA 39)

5. **Temporal Cortex**
   - Superior Temporal Cortex (BA 13, 38)
   - Lateral Temporal Cortex (BA 20, 37)
   - Mediotemporal Cortex
     - Hippocampus
     - Parahippocampal Gyrus (BA 28)
   - Anterior Hippocampus

6. **Occipital Cortex**
   - Occipital parts of BA 39 and BA 19

7. **Thalamus**

8. **Cingulate Cortex**

9. **Cerebellum**
   - Posterior Cerebellum

10. **Brainstem**
    - Mesencephalon
    - Metencephalon (Pons)
    - Rostral Raphe Nuclei

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including memory, executive functions, and emotional processing.
