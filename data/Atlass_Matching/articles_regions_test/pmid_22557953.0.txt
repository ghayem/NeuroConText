## Brain Regions and Sub-Regions

1. **Limbic System**
   - Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)
   - Amygdala
   - Hippcampus
   - Thalamus

2. **Inferior Frontal Cortex (IFC)**
   - Right inferior frontal cortex (rIFC)
     - Pars opercularis (specifically, the rIFC)
   - Brodmann Area 47 (BA 47)

3. **Pre-Supplementary Motor Area (pre-SMA)**
   - Dorsomedial frontal cortex (part of pre-SMA)

4. **Basal Ganglia**
   - Sub-thalamic nucleus (STN)
   - Globus pallidus (GP)
     - Lateral globus pallidus (GP lateral)
     - Medial globus pallidus (GP medial)
   - Striatum

5. **Motor Cortex (MC)**
   - Brodmann Area 4 (BA 4)
   - Brodmann Area 6 (BA 6)

6. **Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)**
   - Brodmann Area 8 (BA 8)
   - Brodmann Area 9 (BA 9)
   - Brodmann Area 10 (BA 10)
   - Brodmann Area 11 (BA 11)
   - Brodmann Area 44 (BA 44)
   - Brodmann Area 45 (BA 45)
   - Brodmann Area 46 (BA 46)
   - Brodmann Area 47 (BA 47)

7. **Posterior Cingulate Cortex**

8. **Temporal Cortex**

9. **Posterior Superior Temporal Gyrus and Sulcus (pSTG/S)**

10. **Middle Superior Temporal Cortex**

11. **Anterior Portion of the pSTG/S**

### Cognitive Functions:
- **Limbic System**: Involved in reward-signal processing, emotional responses, and drug-seeking behavior.
- **Inferior Frontal Cortex (IFC)**: Key area for inhibitory control, specifically the rIFC.
- **Pre-Supplementary Motor Area (pre-SMA)**: Involved in generating control signals for specific actions.
- **Basal Ganglia**: Involved in inhibitory processes and motor control.
- **Motor Cortex (MC)**: Involved in the execution of motor actions.
- **Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)**: Involved in response selection and higher cognitive functions.
- **Posterior Cingulate Cortex**: Involved in context and memory assessment.
- **Temporal Cortex**: Involved in language and memory functions.

These regions and their sub-regions are crucial for various cognitive functions, including emotional processing, executive control, motor inhibition, and higher cognitive processes.
