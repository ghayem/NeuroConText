# Brain regions and sub-regions:

1. **Primary visual cortex**
   - V1

2. **Visual cortex**
   - Area V2/V3
   - Area V3A
   - Calcarine sulcus

3. **Visual cortex (specific regions)**
   - V1v (ventral V1)
   - V1d (dorsal V1)
   - V2v/VP (ventral V2/VP)
   - V2d/V3 (dorsal V2/V3)

4. **Motion-sensitive higher areas**
   - MT+/V5

5. **Retinotopic maps**
   - Retinotopic areas corresponding to the illusory part of the trajectory

6. **Regions of interest (ROIs)**
   - ROIs in retinotopic maps abutting the start or end point of the motion trajectory

7. **Occipital cortex**
   - Left occipital cortex

8. **Cortical surface reconstruction**

9. **Higher areas**

10. **Motion-sensitive area**
    - V3A

11. **Ventral and dorsal visual cortex**
    - Along the V2/V3 borders

12. **Motion complex**
    - Human motion complex MT+/V5

13. **Visual field**
    - Upper right quadrant
    - Horizontal meridian

14. **Motion trajectory**
    - Start/end position of the bar's trajectory

15. **Retinotopic localization**
    - Retinotopic localization of regions of interest (ROIs) in visual cortex

### Additional Areas:
- Superior temporal plane (STP)
- Planum temporale (PT)
- Posterior superior temporal gyrus and sulcus (pSTG/S)
- Temporo-parieto-occipital junction (TPO)

These regions are involved in these cognitive functions: visual perception, motion perception, predictive coding, and visual attention.
