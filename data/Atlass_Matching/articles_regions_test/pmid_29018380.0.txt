Based on the neuroscientific article provided, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned:

1. **Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)**
   - Dorsolateral PFC (DLPFC)
   - Dorsomedial PFC (DMPFC)
   - Ventromedial PFC (VMPFC)
   - Orbitofrontal cortex (OFC)
   - Inferior Frontal Gyrus (IFG)
   - Middle Frontal Gyrus (MFG)
   - Superior Frontal Gyrus (SFG)
   - Frontal pole
   - Precentral gyrus
   - Juxtapositional lobule cortex (formerly pre-motor cortex)

2. **Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC)**
   - Paracingulate cortex

3. **Limbic System**
   - Amygdala
   - Ventral striatum
   - Nucleus accumbens

4. **Temporal Cortex**
   - Temporal pole
   - Inferior temporal gyrus
   - Middle temporal gyrus
   - Superior temporal gyrus

5. **Occipital Cortex**
   - Occipital pole
   - Lateral occipital cortex
   - Occipital fusiform cortex

6. **Parietal Cortex**
   - Precuneus
   - Posterior cingulate cortex

7. **Cerebellum**

8. **Basal Ganglia**
   - Subcortical regions

9. **Other Regions**
   - Cuneus
   - Thalamus
   - Hippocampus

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including but not limited to:
- Executive functions (PFC, ACC)
- Emotion processing (Limbic system, OFC, VMPFC)
- Visual processing (Occipital cortex)
- Memory (Temporal cortex, Hippocampus)
- Motor control (Cerebellum, Precentral gyrus)
- Reward processing (Ventral striatum, Nucleus accumbens)
