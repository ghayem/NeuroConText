Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the neuroscientific article:

1. **Frontal Lobe**
   - Bilateral frontal lobe
   - Brodmann area 10 (BA 10)
   - Brodmann area 11 (BA 11)
   - Brodmann area 25 (BA 25)
   - Brodmann area 47 (BA 47)

2. **Temporal Lobe**
   - Bilateral temporal lobe
   - Brodmann area 22 (BA 22)
   - Brodmann area 30 (BA 30)
   - Brodmann area 38 (BA 38)
   - Fusiform gyrus (FG; BA 18)

3. **Cingulate Gyrus**
   - Anterior cingulate gyrus
   - Inferior cingulate gyrus
   - Brodmann area 32 (BA 32)

4. **Caudate Nucleus**

5. **Occipital Lobe**

6. **Lenticular Nucleus**

7. **Thalamus**
   - Bilateral thalamus
   - Left thalamus

8. **Cerebellum**
   - Cerebellar vermis
   - Gray matter of the cerebellum

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including but not limited to:
- **Frontal Lobe**: Body movement, language, working memory, deductive reasoning, affective prosody, risk and decision making, odor evaluation, reward and conflict, pain.
- **Temporal Lobe**: Auditory, olfactory, vestibular, visual, and linguistic functions.
- **Cingulate Gyrus**: Cognition, specifically executive functioning.
- **Caudate Nucleus**: Executive functions, memory, emotion, and language.
- **Occipital Lobe**: Visual processing.
- **Lenticular Nucleus**: Motor control.
- **Thalamus**: Cognitive functions including memory and executive functions of attention and information processing.
- **Cerebellum**: Motor and cognitive behavior.
