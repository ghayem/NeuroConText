Certainly! Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Amygdala**

2. **Insula**

3. **Dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC)**

4. **Prefrontal cortex (PFC)**
   - Dorsolateral PFC (DLPFC)
   - Ventrolateral PFC (VLPFC)
   - Medial PFC (MPFC)
   - Ventromedial PFC (VMPFC)
   - Dorsomedial PFC (DMPFC)
   - Rostral ACC (rACC)
   - Subgenual ACC (sgACC)

5. **Orbitofrontal cortex (OFC)**
   - Lateral OFC (LOFC)
     - BA 47
     - Lateral BA 11
   - Medial OFC (MOFC)
     - BA 11

6. **Hippocampus**

7. **Thalamus**
   - Mediodorsal nucleus (MD)

8. **Locus coeruleus**

9. **Parahippocampal gyrus (PHG)**
   - Right amygdala/PHG

10. **Precuneus (PCun)**
    - Right posterior cingulate/precuneus

11. **Parietal regions**
    - Inferior parietal lobule

12. **Caudate**
    - Left caudate

13. **Lingual gyrus (LinG)**
    - Left lingual gyrus

14. **Temporal regions**
    - Left superior temporal gyrus (BA 38, temporal pole)
    - Left inferior temporal gyrus (ITG, BA 20)

15. **Frontopolar cortex**
    - Medial frontopolar cortex (BA 9)

16. **Precentral gyrus**
    - Left precentral gyrus (BA 4)

17. **Angular gyrus**
    - Right angular gyrus

18. **Intraparietal sulcus**
    - Left horizontal segment of the intraparietal sulcus (LHIPS)

These regions are involved in various cognitive and emotional functions, including threat detection, cognitive and emotion regulation, contextual processing, memory functions, and executive functions.
