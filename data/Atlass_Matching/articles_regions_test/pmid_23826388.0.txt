Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Medial Frontal Cortex**

2. **Parietal Cortex**
    - Left inferior parietal cortex/posterior part of the superior temporal gyrus (LIPC)
    - Right inferior parietal cortex/posterior part of the superior temporal gyrus (RIPC)

3. **Hippocampal Areas**
    - Left hippocampal and parahippocampal complex (LPHC)
    - Right hippocampal and parahippocampal complex (RPHC)

4. **Thalamic Regions**

5. **Cingulate Cortex Regions**
    - Anterior cingulate cortex/ventromedial prefrontal cortex (ACC)
    - Posterior cingulate cortex/precuneus (PCC)
    - Subgenual part of the ACC

6. **Midbrain Raphe Nucleus**

7. **Amygdala**

8. **Lateral Prefrontal Cortex**

9. **Posterior Parietal Cortex**

10. **Left Middle Frontal Gyrus (LMFG)**

11. **Cerebellar Matter**

12. **Ventricular Space**

13. **Subcortical Limbic System Areas**

14. **Temporal Cortex**

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including social and self-referential perception, internal mental representation, memory, affective processing, sustained attention, and alertness.
