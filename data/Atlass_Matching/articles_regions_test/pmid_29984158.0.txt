Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Frontal Lobe**
    - Orbitofrontal cortex (BA 10, BA 11)
    - Middle frontal cortex
    - Right primary motor regions

2. **Temporal Lobe**
    - Anterior temporal brain regions
    - Right temporal pole (BA 38)

3. **Parietal Lobe**
    - Posterior cingulate
    - Precuneus

4. **Occipital Lobe**
    - Left occipital cortex (BA 18)

5. **Insula**
    - Anterior insula

6. **Limbic System**

7. **White Matter (WM) Regions**
    - Superior longitudinal fasciculus (SLF)
    - Corticospinal tract (CST)
    - Inferior longitudinal fasciculus (ILF)
    - Inferior fronto-occipital fasciculus (IFO)
    - Cingulum
    - Corpus callosum
    - Uncinate fasciculus
    - Frontal corpus callosum
    - Splenium of corpus callosum

8. **Additional Subcortical Areas**
    - Subcortical gray matter (GM)
    - Cortical GM
    - Brainstem
    - Cerebellum

These regions are involved in cognitive functions such as behavior, language, executive functions, and memory, which are affected in frontotemporal degeneration (FTD).
