Certainly! Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the neuroscientific article:

1. **Medial Temporal Lobe (MTL)**
   - Hippocampus
   - Entorhinal cortex
   - Parahippocampal gyrus
   - Parasubiculum
   - Presubiculum
   - Subiculum
   - CA1
   - CA3 (including CA2)
   - CA4
   - Granulate cell of the molecular layer of dentate gyrus
   - Hippocampal molecular layer
   - Hippocampal fissure
   - Fimbria
   - Hippocampus-amygdala transition area

2. **Temporo-Parietal Cortex**

3. **Frontal Lobe**
   - Triangular part of the inferior frontal gyrus
   - Orbital part of the middle frontal gyrus
   - Superior frontal gyrus
   - Middle frontal gyrus
   - Superior medial frontal gyrus
   - Orbital part of the medial frontal gyrus
   - Precentral gyrus
   - Lateral orbitofrontal cortex

4. **Parietal Lobe**
   - Postcentral gyrus
   - Inferior parietal lobe
   - Precuneus

5. **Cingulate Cortex**
   - Anterior cingulate cortex
   - Middle cingulate cortex

6. **Temporal Lobe**
   - Inferior temporal gyrus
   - Middle temporal gyrus
   - Fusiform gyrus

7. **Occipito-Temporal Areas**

8. **Subcortical Structures**
   - Thalamus
   - Corona radiata
   - Cholinergic basal nuclei

9. **Other Regions**
   - Bilateral insula
   - Precentral/postcentral gyrus
   - Left precentral/postcentral gyrus
   - Left triangular part of the inferior frontal gyrus
   - Left precentral/postcentral gyrus
   - Right middle temporal gyrus
   - Left entorhinal cortex
   - Right lateral orbitofrontal cortex

10. **AD Signature Index Regions**
    - Entorhinal cortex
    - Inferior temporal gyrus
    - Middle temporal gyrus
    - Inferior parietal lobe
    - Fusiform gyrus
    - Precuneus

These regions are involved in these cognitive functions: executive function, attention, episodic memory, language, successful episodic memory retrieval, and cognitive control processes (e.g., retrieval monitoring).
