Certainly! Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the neuroscientific article:

1. **Cerebellum**
   - Sensorimotor regions (right lobule I-IV and V, right crus I&II, vermis VI and VIIIa, left lobules I-IV and V)
   - Dentate nucleus

2. **Thalamus**
   - Posterior ventral lateral nucleus (VLp, also referred to as VIM)
   - Ventral lateral anterior nucleus (VLa, also referred to as VOp)
   - Cerebellar relay nucleus

3. **Basal Ganglia**
   - Internal globus pallidus (GPi)
   - Thalamic relay nuclei (VLa)

4. **Primary Motor Cortex**
   - Brodmann Area 4 (BA4)
   - Hand area of the primary motor cortex

5. **Frontal Lobe**
   - Frontal pole

6. **Brainstem**

7. **Temporal Lobe**
   - Temporal pole

8. **Sensorimotor Cortex**

These regions are involved in the pathophysiology of dystonic tremor syndromes, including motor control, tremor regulation, and sensorimotor integration.
