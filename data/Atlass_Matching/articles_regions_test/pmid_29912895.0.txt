Based on the neuroscientific article provided, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned:

1. **Dorsolateral Prefrontal Cortex (dlPFC)**

2. **Intraparietal Sulcus (IPS)**

3. **Anterior Insular Cortex (aIC)**
    - Left anterior Insular Cortex ( left aIC)
    - Bilateral anterior Insular Cortex (bilateral aIC)
    - Left operculum (extending from left aIC)

4. **Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC)**
    - Dorsal Anterior Cingulate Cortex (dorsal ACC)
    - Anterior Midcingulate Cortex (based on a more recent receptor-based neurobiological model)
    - Left dorsal Anterior Cingulate Cortex (left dorsal ACC)

5. **Striatum**
    - Dorsal striatum
    - Left lateral caudate nucleus (part of dorsal striatum)
    - Caudate nucleus (stretching across the internal capsule to the putamen)
    - Bilateral striatal/thalamic areas

6. **Middle Frontal Gyrus (MFG)**
    - Left Middle Frontal Gyrus (left MFG)
    - Bilateral Middle Frontal Gyrus (bilateral MFG)

7. **Angular Gyrus**

8. **Precentral Gyrus/Primary Motor Cortex (M1)**

9. **Posterior Medial Superior Frontal Gyrus (SFG)**

10. **Lateral Superior Frontal Gyrus (SFG)**
    - Left lateral Superior Frontal Gyrus (left lateral SFG)

11. **Frontal Eye Field (FEF)**
    - Located in the posterior medial Superior Frontal Gyrus (SFG)

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including mental effort investment, task management, performance monitoring, cognitive control, and reward/cost evaluations.
