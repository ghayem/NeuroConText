Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Precuneus**

2. **Posterior Cingulate Cortex (PCC)**

3. **Lingual Gyrus**
    - Bilateral lingual gyrus

4. **Superior Temporal Gyrus**
    - Bilateral superior temporal gyrus
    - Right superior temporal gyrus

5. **Inferior Frontal Gyrus (IFG)**

6. **Supplementary Motor Area (SMA)**
    - Right SMA

7. **Cuneus**

8. **Supramarginal Gyrus**

9. **Postcentral Gyrus**

10. **Medial Temporal Pole**

11. **Superior Medial Gyrus**

12. **Middle Frontal Gyrus**
    - Left middle frontal gyrus

13. **Medial Frontal Gyrus**
    - Anterior medial frontal gyrus

14. **Insula**

15. **Inferior Parietal Lobule**

16. **Thalamus**

17. **Caudate**

18. **Amygdala**
    - Right amygdala

19. **Default Mode Network (DMN)**
    - Includes subnetworks highly interconnected to cognitive processes involved in behavioral therapy

20. **Fronto-parietal Network**

21. **Cingulo-opercular Network**

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including but not limited to:
- Self-awareness and self-reflection (precuneus, PCC)
- Language processing (lingual gyrus, superior temporal gyrus, IFG, SMA)
- Memory and understanding others' minds (DMN)
- Executive control and attention (middle frontal gyrus, medial frontal gyrus)
- Emotion processing (amygdala, insula)
- Social cognition and comprehension (superior temporal gyrus, supramarginal gyrus)
- Motor planning and coordination (postcentral gyrus, SMA)
- Visual processing (cuneus, lingual gyrus)
- Decision-making and reward processing (caudate, thalamus)
