Based on the provided article, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned:

1. **Prefrontal Cortex**
   - Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex
   - Medial frontal gyrus (BA 10)
   - Superior frontal gyrus (SFG) (BA 8)
   - Middle frontal gyrus (MFG) (BA 6)

2. **Temporal Cortex**
   - Middle temporal gyrus (MTG) (BA 39)
   - Superior temporal gyrus (STG) (BA 39, BA 21, BA 22)
   - Inferior temporal cortex

3. **Parietal Cortex**
   - Inferior parietal lobule (IPL) (BA 40)
   - Superior parietal lobule (BA 7)
   - Postcentral gyrus (BA 7)

4. **Limbic System**
   - Amygdala
   - Hippocampus
   - Parahippocampal gyrus (PHG) (BA 36)
   - Cingulate cortex (BA 32, BA 31)
   - Posterior cingulate (BA 29)
   - Anterior cingulate

5. **Occipital Cortex**
   - Middle occipital gyrus (BA 19)
   - Cuneus (BA 7)

6. **Insula** (BA 13)

7. **Cerebellum**
   - Culmen
   - Posterior cerebellar hemispheres
   - Lobules I-IV/V, VI/Crus I/II, VIII

8. **Basal Ganglia**
   - Caudate
   - Lentiform nucleus

9. **Other Regions**
   - Precuneus (BA 7)
   - Fusiform gyrus (BA 19)
   - Precentral gyrus (BA 6)
   - Planum temporale (PT)
   - Occipital face area (OFA)
   - Fusiform face area (FFA)
   - Superior temporal sulcus area (pSTS-FA)

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including emotion processing, memory, sensory processing, cognitive control, and self-referential processing. They are also part of different networks like the default mode network (DMN), salience network (SN), and executive control network (ECN).
