Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the neuroscientific article:

1. **Primary Visual Cortex (V1)**
   - Also known as Brodmann Area 17

2. **Salience Network**
   - Anterior insula
   - Dorsal anterior cingulate cortex

3. **Default Mode Network**
   - Inferior parietal cortex
   - Ventromedial frontal cortex
   - Posterior cingulate/precuneus

4. **Sensorimotor Network**
   - Postcentral gyrus
   - Precentral gyrus

5. **Visual Areas**
   - Superior occipital cortex
   - Inferior occipital cortex
   - Lateral occipital cortex

6. **Frontal Cortex**
   - Dorsolateral frontal cortex
     - Superior frontal gyrus
     - Middle frontal gyrus
     - Inferior frontal gyrus
   - Dorsomedial frontal cortex
   - Ventromedial prefrontal cortex

7. **Temporal Cortex**
   - Superior temporal cortex
   - Middle temporal cortex
   - Inferior temporal cortex

8. **Cerebellum**

9. **Lingual Gyrus**

10. **Cuneus**

11. **Supplementary Motor Area**

12. **Thalamus**

13. **Hippocampus/Parahippocampus**

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions such as attention, vigilance, mental imagery, introspection, and sensory processing.
