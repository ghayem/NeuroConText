Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Frontal Cortex**
   - Prefrontal cortex
     - Middle frontal gyrus
     - Medial prefrontal cortex
     - Cingulate cortex
   - Superior frontal cortex

2. **Sensorimotor Cortices**
   - Pre- and postcentral gyri
   - Sensorimotor cortex

3. **Temporal Cortex**
   - Superior temporal cortex
     - Superior temporal gyrus
   - Middle temporal lobe
   - Inferior temporal lobe
   - Insula

4. **Parietal Cortex**
   - Supramarginal gyrus
   - Superior parietal cortex

5. **Occipital Cortex**
   - Posterior cingulate gyrus

6. **Cerebellum**
   - Anterior and superior-posterior lobes
   - Vermis
   - Bilateral crus I

7. **Basal Ganglia**
   - Thalamus
   - Striatum
   - Globus pallidus

8. **Hippocampus**
   - Anterior hippocampus
   - Posterior hippocampus
   - Parahippocampal cortex

9. **White Matter Regions**
   - Superior corona radiata
     - Superior longitudinal fasciculus
     - Corticospinal tract
   - Corpus callosum
     - Splenium
   - Internal capsule
   - Thalamic radiations

10. **Ventricles**
    - Lateral ventricles

11. **Other Regions**
    - Temporo-parieto-occipital junction (TPO)
    - Planum temporale (PT)
    - Central sulcus
    - Lateral fissure

These regions are involved in various cognitive and motor functions, including but not limited to:
- **Frontal Cortex**: Executive functions, motor control, language, and working memory.
- **Sensorimotor Cortices**: Motor and sensory processing.
- **Temporal Cortex**: Auditory processing, language comprehension, memory, and multimodal sensory integration.
- **Parietal Cortex**: Spatial awareness, navigation, and sensory integration.
- **Occipital Cortex**: Visual processing.
- **Cerebellum**: Motor control, coordination, and cognitive functions.
- **Basal Ganglia**: Motor control, procedural learning, and reward processing.
- **Hippocampus**: Memory consolidation and spatial navigation.
- **White Matter Regions**: Communication between different brain regions.
- **Ventricles**: Cerebrospinal fluid circulation and brain volume regulation.
