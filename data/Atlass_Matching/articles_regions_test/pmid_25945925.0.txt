Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the neuroscientific article:

1. **Frontal Lobe**
   - Frontal pole (FP)
     - Lateral division
     - Medial division
     - Orbital surface
     - Fp1 (lateral cytoarchitectonic area)
     - Fp2 (medial cytoarchitectonic area)
   - Prefrontal cortex (PFC)
     - Dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC)
     - Ventral prefrontal cortex
     - Medial prefrontal cortex
     - Orbital prefrontal cortex
   - Inferior frontal gyrus (IFG)
   - Frontal eye fields

2. **Parietal Lobe**
   - Lateral parietal cortex
   - Inferior parietal lobule
   - Superior parietal cortex
   - Posterior parietal cortex

3. **Temporal Lobe**
   - Temporal pole
   - Amygdala
   - Hippocampus
   - Posterior superior temporal sulcus
   - Inferior temporal gyrus
   - Fusiform gyrus
   - Extrastriate cortex

4. **Occipital Lobe**
   - Visual cortex
   - Lateral occipital cortex (BA39/Angular Gyrus)

5. **Cingulate Cortex**
   - Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)
     - Subgenual ACC
     - Pregenual ACC
   - Posterior cingulate cortex

6. **Insula**
   - Anterior insula

7. **Subcortical Structures**
   - Thalamus
     - Anterior thalamic radiation (ATR)

8. **White Matter Tracts**
   - Inferior fronto-occipital fasciculus (iFOF)
   - Uncinate fasciculus (UF)
   - Cingulum bundle (CB)
   - Superior longitudinal fasciculus (SLF)

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including but not limited to:
- Executive functions ( frontal lobe, prefrontal cortex, DLPFC, ACC, posterior parietal cortex)
- Language (inferior frontal gyrus, temporal lobe)
- Memory (hippocampus, temporal lobe, prefrontal cortex)
- Emotion regulation (medial prefrontal cortex, ACC, amygdala)
- Attention (anterior insula, ACC, posterior parietal cortex)
- Visual processing (visual cortex, fusiform gyrus, extrastriate cortex)
- Motivation and reward processing (medial prefrontal cortex, orbital prefrontal cortex, amygdala)
