Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Auditory Cortex**
   - Primary auditory cortex (A1, BA 41)
   - Secondary auditory cortex

2. **Ventromedial Prefrontal Cortex (vmPFC)**

3. **Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC)**
   - Anterior insula
   - Rostral ACC
   - Dorsal ACC
   - Pregenual ACC
   - Subgenual ACC (sgACC, BA 25)

4. **Thalamus**
   - Thalamic ventroposterolateral nucleus
   - Thalamic dorsomedial nucleus

5. **Amygdala**

6. **Parietal Cortex**
   - Primary somatosensory cortex
   - Secondary somatosensory cortex

7. **Parahippocampus**

8. **Inferior Colliculi**

9. **Nucleus Accumbens (NAc)**

10. **Periaqueductal Gray**

11. **Tectal Longitudinal Column (TLC)**

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions including auditory processing, emotion regulation, pain perception, and executive functions.
