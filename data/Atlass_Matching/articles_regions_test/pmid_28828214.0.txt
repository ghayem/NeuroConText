Certainly! Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Frontal Lobe**
   - Frontal white matter (WM)
   - Bilateral frontal WM

2. **Temporal Lobe**
   - Temporal white matter
   - Bilateral temporal WM

3. **Parietal Lobe**
   - Parietal white matter
   - Bilateral parietal WM

4. **Occipital Lobe**
   - Occipital white matter
   - Bilateral occipital WM
   - Right occipital WM region

5. **Corpus Callosum**
   - Anterior corpus callosum

6. **Internal Capsule**
   - Right internal capsule
   - Posterior crus of the right internal capsule

7. **External Capsule**
   - Bilateral external capsule

8. **Corticospinal Tract (CST)**
   - Right CST (at the posterior crus of the internal capsule)

9. **Fronto-Striatal Network**
   - Fronto-striatal circuits

10. **Supplemental Motor Area**

11. **Cingulate Cortex**

12. **Insula**

13. **Response Inhibition Network**

14. **Optic Radiation**
   - Right optic radiation

15. **Inferior Longitudinal Fasciculus**
   - Left inferior longitudinal fasciculus

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions including impulse control, attention, emotion regulation, and motor control.
