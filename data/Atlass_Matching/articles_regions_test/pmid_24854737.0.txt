Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **White matter**
   - Deep white matter
   - Subcortical white matter
   - Parieto-occipital white matter

2. **Grey matter**
   - Occipital grey matter
   - Basal ganglia
   - Pons

3. **Frontal lobes**
   - Bilateral frontal areas
   - Pre-frontal cortices (PFC)
   - Bilateral medial orbito-prefrontal cortex (OFC)
   - Brodmann's area 10, 11, 32
   - Left and right dorsal lateral prefrontal cortex (dlPFC)
   - Bilateral dorsal lateral prefrontal cortex (dlPFC)

4. **Parietal lobes**
   - Bilateral parietal areas
   - Bilateral superior parietal lobe

5. **Temporal lobes**
   - Bilateral temporal areas
   - Left middle temporal gyrus
   - Left superior temporal gyrus
   - Left transverse temporal gyrus (Brodmann's area 41)
   - Bilateral superior temporal gyrus (Brodmann's area 42)
   - Left inferior temporal gyrus
   - Right inferior temporal gyrus
   - Temporal poles

6. **Occipital lobes**
   - Bilateral occipital lobes
   - Bilateral lingual lobes
   - Bilateral calcarine

7. **Cerebellum**
   - Bilateral posterior cerebellar lobes

8. **Other regions**
   - Caudate nuclei
   - Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)
   - Posterior cingulate cortex/precuneus/cuneus
   - Right fusiform
   - Right parahippocampus
   - Right amygdala
   - Left hippocampus/parahippocampus
   - Left angular gyrus (Brodmann's area 39)
   - Right inferior parietal lobule (Brodmann's area 39)
   - Left premotor cortex (Brodmann's area 6)
   - Uncus

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including concentration, memory, planning, executive functions, decision-making, language processing, and higher-order cognitive functioning.
