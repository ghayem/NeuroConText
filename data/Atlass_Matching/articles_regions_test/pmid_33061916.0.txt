Certainly! Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Somatosensory cortex (SSC)**
   - Sensorimotor Network (SMN)

2. **Insula**
   - Salience Network (SN)

3. **Default Mode Network (DMN)**
   - Midline structures
     - Perigenual anterior cingulate cortex (pgACC)

4. **Executive Network (EN)**
   - Dorsal-lateral prefrontal cortex (DLPFC)

5. **Raphe nuclei**

6. **Ventromedial prefrontal cortex (VMPFC)**

7. **Thalamus**

8. **Precuneus**

9. **Inferior frontal gyrus**
   - Inferior frontal sulcus
   - Horizontal ramus

10. **Post-central gyrus**
    - Brodmann areas 1, 2, and 3

11. **Anterior cingulate cortex**
    - Perigenual anterior cingulate cortex (pgACC)

These regions are involved in various cognitive and emotional processes, including visceral sensory input, affective and interoceptive information integration, and executive functions.
