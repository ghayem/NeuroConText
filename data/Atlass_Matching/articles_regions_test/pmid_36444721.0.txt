Here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Centrotemporal region**
   - This region is associated with focal motor and sensory seizures involving the face and oropharynx.

2. **Frontal lobe**

3. **Temporal lobe**
   - Left temporal pole

4. **Left triangular region**

5. **Brainstem**
   - Right brainstem

6. **Cingulate gyrus**
   - Right middle cingulate gyrus

7. **Thalamus**
   - Right thalamus

8. **Precuneus**
   - Left precuneus

9. **Limbic system**
   - Cingulate gyrus is a part of the limbic system.

10. **Default mode network**
    - Precuneus is a key hub of the default mode network.

11. **Paralimbic region**
    - Temporal pole is a crucial paralimbic region.

These regions are involved in various cognitive functions, including:
- **Centrotemporal region**: Sensorimotor functions, language processing.
- **Frontal lobe**: Executive functions, motor control, language production.
- **Temporal lobe**: Auditory processing, language comprehension, memory.
- **Brainstem**: Regulation of automatic functions (heart rate, breathing), sleep-wake cycle.
- **Cingulate gyrus**: Emotion, sensorimotor functions, cognitive control.
- **Thalamus**: Sensory processing, motor control, consciousness.
- **Precuneus**: Self-referential processing, state of consciousness, visuospatial imagery.
- **Limbic system**: Emotion, memory, motivation.
- **Default mode network**: Self-referential processing, mind-wandering, consciousness.
- **Paralimbic region**: Emotion, motivation, visceral functions.
