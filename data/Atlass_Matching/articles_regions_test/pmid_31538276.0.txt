- These regions are involved in cognitive functions such as memory, motor control, executive functions, and psychomotor speed.

The specific involvement of these regions in cognitive functions is as follows:
- **Fusiform Gyrus**: Visual perception, face recognition.
- **Calcarine**: Primary visual cortex, involved in visual processing.
- **Temporal Gyrus**: Auditory processing, object recognition, memory, language comprehension.
- **Hippocampus**: Memory consolidation, spatial navigation.
- **Caudate Nucleus**: Motor control, learning, memory, reward processing.
- **Thalamus**: Sensory relay, motor control, consciousness.
- **Cingulum**: Emotion formation and processing, learning, memory.
- **Cerebellum**: Motor control, coordination, cognitive functions.
- **Olfactory**: Sense of smell.
- **Lingual Gyrus**: Visual processing, dreaming, encoding visual memories.
- **Rectus Gyrus**: Part of the orbitofrontal cortex, involved in decision-making.
- **Orbital Parts of Superior Frontal Gyrus (ORBsup)**: Decision-making, cognitive control.
- **Frontal Gyrus**: Executive functions, motor control, language production.
