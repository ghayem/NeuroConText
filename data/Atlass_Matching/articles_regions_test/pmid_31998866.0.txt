## Brain Regions and Sub-Regions

1. **Cortical Mantle**

2. **Limbic and Association Cortices**

3. **Cerebral Cortex**

4. **Medial Temporal Lobe (MTL)**
   - Hippocampus
     - This region is involved in memory formation

5. **Grey Matter (GM)**
   - Global cerebrum (CE)
   - Cortical grey matter (CGM)
   - Subcortical grey matter (SGM)
     - Nucleus accumbens
     - Amygdala
     - Caudate
     - Pallidum
     - Putamen
     - Thalamus

6. **White Matter (WM)**

7. **Frontal Lobe**
   - Frontal operculum
   - Precentral gyrus

8. **Parietal Lobe**
   - Precuneus

9. **Temporal Lobe**
   - Fusiform gyrus (bilaterally)
   - Superior temporal gyrus (bilaterally)
   - Middle temporal gyrus (left)
   - Inferior temporal gyrus
   - Temporo-parieto-occipital junction (TPO)

10. **Occipital Lobe**

11. **Orbitofrontal Cortex**

12. **Brainstem**

13. **Cerebellum**

### Involved Cognitive Functions
These regions are involved in various cognitive functions including:
- Memory
- Language
- Executive functions
- Interoceptive awareness
- Fluid intelligence
- Motor functions
- Sensory processing

These regions are also implicated in the pathophysiology and symptomatology of Alzheimer's disease (AD) and other neurodegenerative disorders.
