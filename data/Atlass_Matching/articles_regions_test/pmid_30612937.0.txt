Sure, here are the brain regions and sub-regions mentioned in the article:

1. **Amygdala**

2. **Insula**

3. **Ventral striatum (VS)**
   - Nucleus accumbens (NAcc)
   - Ventral regions of the putamen
   - Ventral regions of the caudate

4. **Dorsal striatum**

5. **Prefrontal cortex (PFC)**
   - Dorsolateral PFC (dlPFC)
   - Ventromedial PFC (vmPFC)
   - Medial orbitofrontal area
   - Ventrolateral PFC (vlPFC)

6. **Anterior cingulate cortex (ACC)**

7. **Pre-supplementary motor area**

8. **Superior/intraparietal lobule**

9. **Superior temporal gyrus (STG)**
   - Middle temporal gyrus (MTG)

10. **Posterior insular cortex (PIC)**

11. **Posterior cingulate cortex (PCC)**

12. **Precuneus**

13. **Cuneal cortex**

14. **Inferior frontal gyrus (IFG)**

15. **Medial frontal gyrus (MFG)**

16. **Inferior temporal gyrus (ITG)**

17. **Inferior parietal lobule (IPL)**

These regions are involved in the following cognitive functions: decision-making, risk assessment, reward processing, response inhibition, and outcome learning.
